1928

Elementary at the private school San Gaggio.



1932

entered the first Galileo high school in Florence.



1940

Finished the Classical State High School Galileo in Florence without taking the maturity exam due to the outbreak of the Second World War Enrolled at the UNIVERSITY OF FLORENCE first in literature, then dropped out in the third year and enrolled in physics.

Jan 15, 1945

With 101/110 graduated with a thesis in astrophysics on Cepheids
After graduation, she started working at the Arcetri Observatory in Florence, where she focused on stellar spectroscopy, her main research field.

Jun 12, 1947

Moved to Milan where she worked in an industry that produced cameras, the company went into crisis, and Margherita returned to her studies.

1948

Vasco Ronchi in Florence gave her a job at an Optics institute, so she decided to resign from the previous company and dedicated herself to the study of Cepheids.

1950

Won the competition for assistant professorship in astronomy, allowing her to continue her studies.

1952

Published her first article, a comparative analysis of about ten BE type stars. She received a scholarship to stay for 6 months at the Institut d'Astrophysique in Paris, where she began independent work, and her results were later published in "Annales d'Astrophysique".

1954

Moved to the Merate Observatory, where she conducted an analysis on the Cepheid Ff Aquilae.


1955

Moved to Utrecht as she won a 6-month scholarship. She received a scholarship to stay at the University of Berkeley where she studied a star a little cooler than the sun.

.

1959

Taught a course in astrophysics and radioastronomy at the University of Bologna.


1963

Had to choose whether to take the chair in Bologna or Trieste and won the competition for the one in Trieste and thus in

Dec 15, 1964

Became a professor of astronomy in Trieste until 1992.



1964

Directed the Astronomical Observatory of Trieste until 1987, being the first woman Italian to lead it and bringing it to international renown.


1974

Was asked to organize the second European congress of the Union International Astronomical.

1978

Founded the bi-monthly magazine L'Astronomia with Corrado Lamberti, achieving a great milestone of 30000 copies.

1982

Started a close collaboration with the astrophysics section of the "International School for Advanced Studies" (SISSA in Trieste).
